5 Energy-Saving Tips That Actually Work in Georgia Homes

Georgia summers are brutal and winters can surprise you. These proven strategies from our BPI-certified team will lower your bills without sacrificing comfort.

By Anderson Heating, Air & Insulation • 8 min read • May 2026

If you've lived in Northwest Georgia for any length of time, you know the pain of sky-high summer electric bills and the frustration of a furnace that can't quite keep up on those rare cold snaps. The good news? Most homes in Calhoun, Dalton, Rome, and the surrounding areas are losing 30-50% of their energy through simple, fixable issues.

At Anderson Heating, Air & Insulation (formerly John Anderson Service Company), we've been helping Georgia homeowners since 1978. We don't just swap equipment — we fix the whole house. Here are five tips that deliver real, measurable results.

1. Start with a Whole-Home Energy Audit

Most homeowners guess where they're losing energy. We measure it. A BPI-certified energy audit uses blower door testing, infrared cameras, and duct leakage testing to find exactly where your home is leaking air and energy.

In our experience, the average Georgia home has enough air leaks to equal a 3-foot by 3-foot hole in the wall. Once we identify the problems, we can prioritize fixes that give the biggest bang for the buck.

2. Prioritize Attic Insulation & Air Sealing

In Georgia, your attic is ground zero for energy loss. Most homes built before 2000 have R-19 or less in the attic. Current recommendations for our climate zone are R-49 to R-60.

But insulation without air sealing is like putting a sweater on over a t-shirt with the windows open. We use advanced air sealing techniques around recessed lights, plumbing penetrations, and the attic hatch to stop the stack effect that pulls conditioned air out of your home.

  • Blown-in cellulose or fiberglass — both excellent choices for Georgia attics
  • Seal all penetrations with spray foam or caulk
  • Install radiant barrier to reflect summer heat
  • Ensure proper attic ventilation to prevent moisture issues

3. Maintain Your HVAC Like It's Your Most Valuable Asset

Your HVAC system is the heart of your home's comfort. Yet most homeowners only think about it when it breaks. Regular maintenance can improve efficiency by 15-20%.

Change your filter every 30-90 days (more often if you have pets). Have a professional tune-up twice a year — once before summer and once before winter. At Anderson, our maintenance plans include priority scheduling, discount on repairs, and a thorough 21-point inspection that catches problems before they become expensive emergencies.

4. Consider a High-Efficiency Heat Pump

Georgia's climate is perfect for modern heat pumps. Today's units work efficiently even when temperatures drop into the 20s. Many of our customers in Calhoun and Dalton are seeing 30-50% savings on their heating bills after switching from old electric furnaces or gas systems.

Pair it with proper insulation and duct sealing, and the savings multiply. We install only high-efficiency models with excellent warranties and offer financing through Optimus (GreenSky & Wisetack) so you can start saving immediately.

5. Don't Forget Ductwork — It's Often the Biggest Culprit

Leaky ducts in unconditioned spaces (attics, crawlspaces) can waste 20-30% of your HVAC's output. In older Georgia homes, this is incredibly common.

Our in-house custom sheet metal shop at 519 Pine Street lets us fabricate perfect-fitting ductwork as part of your complete system. No more forcing round pegs into square holes. We seal every joint with mastic and test for leakage before we leave.
