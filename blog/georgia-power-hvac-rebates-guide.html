How to Get Georgia Power & Federal Rebates for HVAC and Insulation

Most Georgia homeowners are leaving thousands of dollars on the table because they don't know about HER, HEAR, and Georgia Power rebates — or because their HVAC contractor isn't authorized to file the paperwork. Anderson HAI is one of the few certified contractors in NW Georgia.

By Anderson Heating, Air & Insulation • 8 min read • May 2026

If you replaced an HVAC system, added insulation, or installed a heat pump in the last few years without filing for rebates, you may have missed real money. Programs change yearly and the paperwork is intimidating — but the savings are sitting there.

Here's the plain-English version of what's available in Northwest Georgia and how to actually get it.

HER — Home Energy Rating Program

HER is a Georgia program that gives homeowners energy-efficiency rebates after a certified Home Energy Rating by an authorized contractor. Anderson is a certified HER rater.

  • What's rebated: insulation, air sealing, ductwork improvements, weatherization
  • Typical rebate range: $500 – $3,500 depending on scope
  • Requirement: certified rater performs a blower-door test and energy audit

HEAR — Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates

HEAR is a federal program (Inflation Reduction Act) administered through Georgia. Larger rebates than HER, especially for heat pump conversions.

  • Heat pump installation: up to $8,000 (income-qualified)
  • Heat pump water heater: up to $1,750
  • Electrical panel upgrade: up to $4,000
  • Insulation, air sealing, ventilation: up to $1,600

Income limits apply for the higher tiers. Mid-income households often still qualify for partial rebates.

Georgia Power Rebates

Georgia Power runs its own rebate program for residential customers. Anderson is an authorized partner contractor.

  • High-efficiency AC and heat pump rebates — typically $300 – $1,200
  • Smart thermostat rebates — small but adds up
  • Whole-home energy assessment — sometimes free for qualifying homes

The Anderson Difference:

We do the paperwork. Most HVAC contractors hand you a brochure and say "good luck." We handle the application, the documentation, the blower-door test, the inspection, and the submission. The rebate hits your account weeks or months after the install — without you doing the legwork.

How the Process Actually Works

  1. Call us — we identify which programs you qualify for based on your home, income, and project scope
  2. Pre-work assessment — energy audit and documentation
  3. Installation — high-efficiency equipment that meets rebate criteria
  4. Post-install verification — final inspection and paperwork
  5. Rebate filing — we submit on your behalf
  6. You get a check or bill credit — typically 4-12 weeks later
