## Calhoun, GA — Gordon County

- **Location:** ~70 miles north of Atlanta on I-75, the seat of Gordon County and the center of Anderson HAI's service area.
- **Elevation:** ~720 ft.
- **Geography:** Sits in the Oostanaula River valley. The Oostanaula forms in Calhoun where the Coosawattee River and Conasauga River meet — meaning Calhoun has river-bottom microclimate features (slightly higher overnight humidity than surrounding higher ground).
- **Climate:** Humid subtropical. July highs avg 88-90°F, January lows avg 28-32°F. Occasional sub-20°F cold snaps but no sustained sub-freezing winters.
- **Building stock:** Historic downtown homes (some pre-1950), mid-century ranch homes, and newer subdivisions on the perimeter. Mix of natural gas and electric heat. Older homes commonly have undersized or modified ductwork from retrofits.
- **Local identity:** Home of Calhoun Magazine, which has named Anderson HAI #1 Heating & Air for 6+ consecutive years.
- **Anderson HAI advantage:** This is home turf — fastest dispatch, lowest travel surcharge, in-house custom HVAC sheet metal shop right here at 519 Pine Street.

## Dalton, GA — Whitfield County

- **Location:** ~22 miles north of Calhoun on I-75. ~25 minutes drive time from Anderson HAI HQ.
- **Elevation:** ~770 ft.
- **Climate:** Humid subtropical, very similar to Calhoun. Slightly higher rainfall.
- **Famous for:** "Carpet Capital of the World" — center of the U.S. residential carpet manufacturing industry. This matters for HVAC: ambient airborne fiber particulates and dust loads are higher in some Dalton neighborhoods than in surrounding cities, which affects filter life and indoor air quality (IAQ).
- **Building stock:** Mix of older mill homes (early-to-mid 20th century, often with cramped utility closets and modified ductwork), and newer subdivisions east of I-75.
- **Local distinction for HVAC:** Higher-than-average IAQ filtration upgrades and duct-cleaning service demand because of the carpet-industry particulates.

## Rome, GA — Floyd County

- **Location:** ~30 miles southwest of Calhoun. ~35-40 minutes drive time via GA-53.
- **Elevation:** ~600 ft.
- **Geography:** Built at the confluence of the Etowah and Oostanaula rivers, which form the Coosa River right in downtown Rome. Three navigable rivers means **significantly higher summer humidity** than other markets — dehumidification matters more here.
- **Climate:** Humid subtropical, with the river-junction effect producing some of the muggiest summer mornings in NW Georgia.
- **Building stock:** Historic neighborhoods (Between the Rivers, Battery Hill) with retrofitted HVAC, often facing undersized return-air ductwork. Berry College and Shorter University areas have higher-end homes that frequently request zoned HVAC systems.
- **Local distinction for HVAC:** Dehumidification and IAQ upgrades are more frequently part of Rome HVAC jobs than in the drier markets. Historic homes often need ductwork redesign as part of HVAC replacement, not just equipment swap.

## Cartersville, GA — Bartow County

- **Location:** ~32 miles south of Calhoun on I-75. ~35-40 minutes drive time.
- **Elevation:** ~787 ft.
- **Climate:** Humid subtropical, transitions into the Atlanta metro climate band.
- **Building stock:** Heavy with 2000s-2010s spec-home subdivisions. These newer homes typically have factory-builder HVAC systems that are right-sized on paper but undersized in practice due to envelope leakage. Pre-2000 homes are a smaller share of the market than in Calhoun or Rome.
- **Local distinction:** Allatoona Lake is just southwest of Cartersville — vacation and second homes around the lake have seasonal HVAC concerns (freeze prevention in winter, dehumidification for vacant periods).
- **Anderson HAI advantage:** Cartersville's growth means more competition, but Anderson HAI's 48-year reputation and BPI certification stand out against builders' default HVAC installers.

## Adairsville, GA — Bartow County

- **Location:** ~17 miles south of Calhoun on I-75. ~20 minutes drive time.

- **Heritage:** Historic Western & Atlantic Railroad town; downtown has 1900s commercial buildings.

## Chatsworth, GA — Murray County

- **Location:** ~25 miles northeast of Calhoun. ~30 minutes drive time via US-411.
- **Elevation:** ~755 ft (the town itself; the surrounding county climbs into the Cohutta range).
- **Climate:** Humid subtropical at the town, transitions to mountain climate as elevation rises in the county. Slightly cooler summer nights than valley markets.
- **Geography:** Murray County sits against the Cohutta Wildlife Management Area and the lower Appalachian foothills.
- **Building stock:** Rural service patterns; longer drives between homes. Some second homes and cabins toward the Cohutta side.
- **Local distinction for HVAC:** Cooler summer night temperatures mean heat pumps perform well year-round here — less reliance on supplemental electric or gas heat than in colder mountain markets like Jasper.

## Jasper, GA — Pickens County

- **Location:** ~45 miles east of Calhoun. ~55-60 minutes drive time via GA-53 to GA-515.
- **Elevation:** ~1,500 ft.
- **Climate:** **Significantly cooler than valley markets** — summer highs typically 5-8°F lower than Calhoun, winter lows can hit sub-15°F during cold snaps.
- **Geography:** Pickens County is in the foothills of the southern Appalachians. Burnt Mountain and Sharp Mountain are landmark features.
- **Building stock:** Mix of full-time residences and second homes / vacation cabins. Many homes built in the 1990s-2010s second-home boom.
- **Local distinction for HVAC: This is cold-climate heat pump territory.** Standard heat pumps lose efficiency below ~35°F; Jasper homes benefit from cold-climate heat pumps rated for sub-zero performance or dual-fuel systems pairing a heat pump with gas/propane backup. Second homes also need freeze-protection winterization between visits.

## Ellijay, GA — Gilmer County

- **Location:** ~55 miles east of Calhoun. ~70-75 minutes drive time via GA-515.
- **Elevation:** ~1,300 ft (town); surrounding terrain climbs higher.
- **Climate:** Mountain humid subtropical. Cooler summers than Calhoun. Winter cold snaps and occasional ice/snow events.
- **Famous for:** "Apple Capital of Georgia" — agricultural ag heritage. Apple orchards mean specific humidity and pollen patterns.
- **Building stock:** Heavy cabin and second-home market. Many homes built 1990s-2010s. Year-round residences mixed with vacation rentals (some via Airbnb/VRBO) that need reliable remote-monitorable HVAC.
- **Local distinction for HVAC:** Like Jasper, cold-climate heat pumps perform well here. Vacation rentals benefit from smart thermostats with remote monitoring (so HVAC can be confirmed running before guests arrive). Outdoor condensing units need elevated pads to clear winter ice/snow.
