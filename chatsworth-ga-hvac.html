Chatsworth HVAC: Murray County Mountain Climate & Cohutta Foothills

Chatsworth's climate is different from the valley towns. Sitting at the foot of the Cohutta Mountains in Murray County, homes here see colder winters than Calhoun or Dalton — temps in the 20s and teens are routine in January, and snow happens. That means heating system sizing matters more, and heat pumps need cold-climate certification to perform at design temps.

The mountain proximity also means rapid weather swings: a 70-degree afternoon can drop to 35 overnight in spring or fall. Systems that aren't properly staged or that have undersized return ducts struggle with these swings, often short-cycling and running up the bill. We perform BPI-certified Manual J load calculations to right-size every system we install in Chatsworth and out toward Eton, Crandall, and the foothills around Fort Mountain State Park.

Chatsworth is about 22 miles east of our Calhoun shop. We service Murray County daily, including emergency work. The combination of cold-climate heat pump expertise, BPI certification, and same-day service is hard to find — most contractors in Murray County are HVAC-only without the energy efficiency credentials.
